## 3. Legal Basis for Processing

### 3.1 Consent (Section 6, DPDP Act)

The primary legal basis for processing your Personal Data is your explicit, freely given, informed, specific, unconditional, and unambiguous **consent**, obtained through a clear affirmative action as required under **Section 6(1) of the DPDP Act**.

Before any processing commences, we shall provide you with a notice ("Consent Notice") in accordance with **Section 5(1) of the DPDP Act**, which shall contain:

- **(a)** A description of the Personal Data sought to be collected and the specific purpose of processing;
- **(b)** The manner in which the Data Principal may exercise rights under Chapter IV of the DPDP Act;
- **(c)** The manner in which the Data Principal may make a complaint to the Data Protection Board of India.

### 3.2 Lawful Purpose (Section 4, DPDP Act)

All processing of your Personal Data shall be carried out only for a **lawful purpose**, as mandated by **Section 4(1) of the DPDP Act**. A purpose shall be deemed lawful if it is not expressly forbidden by law.

### 3.3 Purpose Limitation (Section 5(1)(a), DPDP Act)

We shall process your Personal Data only for the **specific purposes** communicated to you in the Consent Notice and for which your consent has been obtained. We shall not process your Personal Data for any purpose that a reasonable person would not expect, given the context in which such Personal Data was provided.

### 3.4 IT Rules, 2011 — Additional Basis for SPDI

In accordance with **Rule 5(1) of the IT (Reasonable Security Practices) Rules, 2011**, we collect Sensitive Personal Data or Information only with your prior consent, following the provision of a privacy policy that is made available for viewing by the provider of information. The consent is obtained through the use of the App and through explicit consent mechanisms embedded therein.

## 4. What Data We Collect

### 4.1 Sensitive Personal Data or Information (SPDI)

The following categories of data are classified as Sensitive Personal Data or Information under **Rule 3 of the IT (Reasonable Security Practices) Rules, 2011** and constitute special categories of Personal Data under applicable law:

#### 4.1.1 Biometric Data — Facial Photographs

- Facial photographs captured by or uploaded through the App;
- Facial feature vectors and measurements derived from processing of facial images;
- Facial analysis outputs including estimated age indicators, skin health metrics, and facial morphology data.

#### 4.1.2 Health Data — Medical Reports and Health Information

- **Blood test reports** (complete blood count, lipid panels, liver function tests, kidney function tests, thyroid profiles, HbA1c, glucose levels, and other haematological and biochemical parameters);
- **Urine test reports** (urinalysis results, creatinine, microalbumin, and other urological parameters);
- **Abdomen scan reports** (ultrasonography findings, organ measurements, and diagnostic observations);
- **Other health reports** as may be uploaded by the Data Principal;
- **Self-reported health conditions** (pre-existing conditions, chronic diseases, allergies, ongoing treatments);
- **Dietary restrictions** arising from medical conditions (e.g., diabetic dietary requirements, renal dietary restrictions);
- **Telomere length measurements** derived from microscopy image analysis.

#### 4.1.3 Genetic Data

- **Self-reported Single Nucleotide Polymorphism (SNP) genotype data** (e.g., rs429358:CT, rs7412:CC, and other SNP identifiers with allele information);
- **Genetic variant classifications** (e.g., APOE ε4 carrier status);
- **Pharmacogenomic markers** relevant to drug metabolism;
- **Ancestry-informative genetic markers**;
- **DNA sequence reconstructions** derived from facial-genomic analysis models.

> **Note:** We do not perform direct genetic sequencing. Genetic variant information is self-reported by the Data Principal or computationally inferred through statistical models applied to other data inputs (such as facial analysis). Inferred genetic data carries inherent uncertainty and is treated as estimates, not clinical-grade genetic test results.

#### 4.1.4 Physiological and Biometric Health Data

- **Biological age estimates** computed from multiple data sources;
- **Telomere length measurements** from user-provided microscopy images;
- **Disease risk scores** computed from genetic, demographic, and health data inputs.

### 4.2 Regular Personal Data

The following categories of data constitute Personal Data under **Section 2(t) of the DPDP Act** but are not classified as SPDI:

#### 4.2.1 Demographic Data

- **Age** (in years);
- **Biological sex** (male, female, or as specified by the Data Principal);
- **Geographic region** (ancestral or ethnic origin for analysis calibration);
- **Country and State** of residence;
- **Dietary restrictions and preferences** (vegetarian, vegan, non-vegetarian, Jain, Halal, or other dietary patterns not arising from medical conditions).

### 4.3 Technical Data

The following technical data may be incidentally collected during App usage:

- **Device type and model** (for compatibility and rendering purposes);
- **Android operating system version**;
- **App version number**;
- **Server endpoint configuration** (user-configured server URL);
- **App theme preferences** (dark mode, light mode);
- **Crash reports** (if enabled by the user, containing stack traces and error metadata — no Personal Data included).

### 4.4 Data We Do NOT Collect

We expressly confirm that we do **not** collect:

- Device identifiers (IMEI, Android ID, Advertising ID);
- Location data (GPS, network-based, or Wi-Fi-based);
- Contact lists, call logs, or SMS messages;

- Passwords or authentication credentials for external services;
- Data from other applications on your device.

## 5. Purpose of Collection

In compliance with **Section 5(1)(a) of the DPDP Act**, the following are the specific and lawful purposes for which your Personal Data is collected and processed:

### 5.1 Telomere Analysis

- Processing microscopy images to measure telomere lengths;
- Calculating the telomere-to-centromere (T/C) ratio;
- Estimating biological age based on telomere length correlations with chronological age;
- Generating telomere health assessments and trend analysis.

### 5.2 Disease Risk Assessment

- Computing polygenic risk scores based on self-reported genetic variants (SNP genotypes);
- Estimating probabilistic risk for conditions including, but not limited to:
  - Alzheimer's disease and other neurodegenerative conditions;
  - Cardiovascular diseases (coronary artery disease, atrial fibrillation, hypertension);
  - Various forms of cancer (breast, colorectal, prostate, lung, and others);
  - Type 2 diabetes mellitus;
  - Autoimmune conditions;
- Correlating genetic risk factors with demographic data (age, sex, region) for population-adjusted estimates;
- Integrating health report findings with genetic data for comprehensive risk profiling.

### 5.3 Nutritional Recommendations

- Generating personalised nutrition plans based on genetic predispositions, health conditions, dietary restrictions, and demographic factors;
- Creating structured meal plans (breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks) with macronutrient and micronutrient profiles;
- Providing dietary guidance aligned with Indian dietary patterns and regional food availability;
- Tailoring recommendations based on health report findings (e.g., dietary adjustments for elevated cholesterol, blood glucose, or renal parameters).

### 5.4 Facial-Genomic Analysis

- Analysing facial photographs to estimate biological age indicators;
- Deriving ancestry-informative features from facial morphology;
- Generating pharmacogenomic inferences from facial-genomic models;
- Assessing skin health metrics (elasticity estimates, pigmentation analysis, UV damage indicators);
- Performing computational DNA sequence reconstruction from facial analysis (research-grade statistical inference);
- Correlating facial features with known genetic variant associations.

### 5.5 Health Checkup Analysis

- Parsing and extracting values from uploaded health reports (blood tests, urine tests, abdomen scans);
- Identifying out-of-range values and flagging potential areas of concern;
- Correlating health report findings with genetic risk factors for integrated risk assessment;
- Generating health trend analyses and actionable insights;
- Providing contextual explanations for medical parameters in accessible language.

### 5.6 No Secondary or Incompatible Processing

We shall **not** process your Personal Data for any purpose not specified herein. In particular, your data shall **not** be used for:

- Advertising, marketing, or promotional purposes;
- Profiling for the purpose of automated decision-making that produces legal effects concerning you;
- Training machine learning models on your individual data without separate, explicit consent;
- Sale, licensing, or commercial transfer to any third party;
- Any purpose that a reasonable person would consider incompatible with the stated purposes above.

### 6.3 Right to Withdraw Consent (Section 6(6), DPDP Act)

## 7. Health Report Upload — Special Provisions

Given the particularly sensitive nature of health and medical data, the following special provisions apply to the upload and processing of health reports:

### 7.1 What Happens When You Upload a Health Report

When you upload a health report (blood test, urine test, abdomen scan, or other medical document) through the App:

1. **Transmission**: The report file is transmitted from your device to the Teloscopy backend server over an encrypted connection;
2. **Parsing**: The server-side processing engine parses the report to extract relevant medical parameters and values;
3. **Extraction**: Individual test values, reference ranges, and units are extracted and structured into a machine-readable format;
4. **Review**: The extracted values are presented to you within the App interface for **your review and confirmation** before any analysis is performed — you have the opportunity to correct any incorrectly parsed values;
5. **Analysis**: Only after your confirmation, the extracted values are used in conjunction with your other data to generate health insights and risk assessments;
6. **Disposal**: The original report file and extracted data are disposed of upon completion of the processing session — they are not written to persistent storage.

### 7.2 How We Parse and Process Reports

- Reports are processed using optical character recognition (OCR) and natural language processing (NLP) techniques where applicable;
- Structured report formats (PDF tables, digital lab results) are parsed programmatically;
- We extract only clinically relevant parameters necessary for the stated purposes;
- All parsing occurs in server memory (RAM) and is not written to disk at any stage.

### 7.3 User Review Before Analysis

In accordance with the principles of transparency and informed consent:

- **No health report analysis shall commence without your explicit review and confirmation of the extracted values**;
- You shall have the opportunity to verify each extracted parameter against your original report;
- You may correct, add, or remove values before proceeding with analysis;
- This step ensures that you retain full control over the health data used in your analysis.

### 7.4 No Persistent Storage of Health Reports

- The uploaded report file is held in server memory **only for the duration of the processing session**;
- No copies of the report are made, backed up, or archived;
- The extracted health data is not stored in any database, file system, or cloud storage;
- Once the processing session concludes (i.e., results are delivered to your device), all report data is purged from server memory;
- We do not retain any health report data beyond the active processing session.

### 7.5 ICMR Guidelines Compliance

While Teloscopy does not conduct biomedical research on human participants, we voluntarily adopt the following principles from the **ICMR National Ethical Guidelines for Biomedical and Health Research (2017)** with regard to health data:

- **Autonomy and Informed Consent**: Health data is processed only with your informed, voluntary consent;
- **Beneficence**: Health data processing is undertaken solely for your benefit (personalised health insights);
- **Non-maleficence**: We implement safeguards to prevent misuse, unauthorised access, or harm arising from health data processing;
- **Confidentiality**: Health data is treated with the highest level of confidentiality and is not disclosed to any third party;
- **Data Minimisation**: We extract and process only the minimum health parameters necessary for the stated purposes.

## 8. Data Storage & Retention

### 8.1 Obligations Under the DPDP Act

In accordance with **Section 8(7) of the DPDP Act**, we shall not retain your Personal Data beyond the period necessary to fulfil the purpose for which it was collected, unless retention is required for compliance with any applicable law.

### 8.2 Storage Architecture

#### 8.2.1 On-Device Storage

- **App preferences** (theme, server configuration): Stored locally on your device using Android DataStore or SharedPreferences. This data remains on your device and is under your exclusive control;
- **Cached analysis results**: Temporarily displayed within the App and cleared when you navigate away from the results screen or close the App;
- **No health data, genetic data, or biometric data is persistently stored on your device** by the App.

#### 8.2.2 Server-Side Processing

- **Facial images**: Processed entirely in server memory (RAM). Images are **not** written to disk, databases, or any persistent storage medium;
- **Health reports**: Processed entirely in server memory. Reports and extracted data are **not** written to disk;
- **Genetic variant data**: Processed in memory for the duration of the analysis request. Not persisted;
- **Demographic and dietary data**: Processed in memory for the duration of the analysis request. Not persisted;
- **Telomere images**: Processed in memory for measurement extraction. Not persisted.

### 8.3 Retention Periods

| Data Category | Retention Period | Basis |
|--------------|-----------------|-------|
| Facial images | Duration of single analysis session (seconds to minutes) | Purpose fulfilment |
| Health reports | Duration of single analysis session | Purpose fulfilment |
| Genetic variant data | Duration of single analysis session | Purpose fulfilment |
| Demographic data | Duration of single analysis session | Purpose fulfilment |
| Analysis results | Until you navigate away from results or close the App (on-device only) | User control |
| App preferences | Until you clear App data or uninstall | User control |
| Consent records | As required by law | Legal obligation |
| Server logs (if enabled) | Maximum 30 days, with anonymisation | Legitimate operational interest, security |

### 8.4 Automatic Deletion

- Analysis data is automatically purged from server memory upon completion of the processing request;
- No manual deletion request is necessary for analysis data, as it is not retained;
- Consent records and server logs (if any) shall be automatically deleted upon expiry of their respective retention periods;
- You may delete on-device data at any time by clearing the App's data through your device settings or by uninstalling the App.

## 9. Data Security

### 9.1 Obligations Under the DPDP Act and IT Rules

In accordance with **Section 8(5) of the DPDP Act**, we implement reasonable security safeguards to prevent Personal Data breaches. Additionally, under **Rule 8 of the IT (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules, 2011**, we maintain a comprehensive documented information security programme that contains managerial, technical, operational, and physical security control measures commensurate with the sensitivity of the information being protected and the nature of the business.

### 9.2 Technical Security Measures

#### 9.2.1 Encryption in Transit

- All data transmitted between the App and the backend server is encrypted using **HTTPS (TLS 1.2 or higher)**;
- Certificate validation is enforced on the client side to prevent man-in-the-middle attacks;
- For self-hosted deployments, we strongly recommend configuring valid TLS certificates.

#### 9.2.2 In-Memory Processing

- All Sensitive Personal Data (facial images, health reports, genetic data) is processed entirely **in server memory (RAM)**;
- No SPDI is written to disk, temporary files, swap storage, or any persistent medium during processing;
- Memory allocated for processing is released and overwritten upon completion of each analysis session.

#### 9.2.3 No Persistent Storage of Health Data

- The server architecture is designed to be **stateless** with respect to user health data;
- No database, file system, object storage, or data lake is used to store SPDI;
- Each analysis request is treated as an independent, isolated processing event.

#### 9.2.4 Access Controls

- Backend server access is restricted to authorised administrators;
- The application does not maintain user accounts, thereby eliminating risks associated with credential storage and authentication breaches;
- API endpoints are secured against unauthorised access;
- For self-hosted deployments, administrators are responsible for configuring appropriate network-level access controls (firewalls, VPNs, etc.).

### 9.3 Organisational Security Measures

- Regular review and updating of security practices in line with industry standards;
- Adherence to **IS/ISO/IEC 27001** principles for information security management, as contemplated under **Rule 8 of the IT Rules, 2011**;
- The open-source nature of Teloscopy enables community-driven security auditing and vulnerability disclosure.

### 9.4 Security Standards Compliance (Rule 8, IT Rules, 2011)

In accordance with Rule 8 of the IT (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules, 2011, we implement security practices and standards that are commensurate with the information assets being protected. Our security programme is aligned with **IS/ISO/IEC 27001** on Information Technology — Security Techniques — Information Security Management System — Requirements, or such other standards as may be notified by the Central Government.

## 10. Your Rights as a Data Principal

Under **Chapter IV (Sections 11–14) of the DPDP Act**, you have the following rights with respect to your Personal Data:

### 10.1 Right to Access Information (Section 11, DPDP Act)

You have the right to obtain from us:

- **(b)** The identities of all Data Fiduciaries and Data Processors with whom your Personal Data has been shared, along with a description of the Personal Data so shared;
- **(c)** Any other information related to your Personal Data and its processing, as may be prescribed.

**How to exercise this right**: Submit a written request to our Grievance Officer at privacy@teloscopy.app. Given that Teloscopy does not persist health data, a summary of our processing practices (as described in this Policy) constitutes the primary response. If specific processing logs are maintained, those shall be provided.

### 10.2 Right to Correction and Erasure (Section 12, DPDP Act)

You have the right to:

- **(a)** **Correction** of inaccurate or misleading Personal Data;
- **(b)** **Completion** of incomplete Personal Data;
- **(c)** **Updating** of Personal Data that is no longer current;
- **(d)** **Erasure** of Personal Data that is no longer necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, unless retention is required by law.

**How to exercise this right**: Given the ephemeral nature of data processing in Teloscopy (no persistent storage), correction and erasure are largely automatic. For any data that may be retained (consent records, server logs), submit a request to privacy@teloscopy.app. On-device data can be erased by clearing the App's data or uninstalling the App.

### 10.3 Right to Grievance Redressal (Section 13, DPDP Act)

You have the right to:

- **(a)** Access a readily available means of registering a grievance with us;
- **(b)** Receive a response to your grievance within such period as may be prescribed;
- **(c)** If not satisfied with our response, lodge a complaint with the **Data Protection Board of India**.

**How to exercise this right**: See [Section 14 (Grievance Redressal)](#14-grievance-redressal) below.

### 10.4 Right to Nominate (Section 14, DPDP Act)

You have the right to **nominate any other individual** who shall, in the event of your death or incapacity, exercise your rights under the DPDP Act. Such nomination may be made by providing written notice to our Grievance Officer at privacy@teloscopy.app.

### 10.5 Duties of Data Principal (Section 15, DPDP Act)

Under **Section 15 of the DPDP Act**, as a Data Principal you also have certain duties, including:

- **(a)** Complying with applicable laws when exercising your rights;
- **(b)** Not impersonating another person or suppressing material information when providing Personal Data;
- **(c)** Not registering a false or frivolous grievance or complaint;
- **(d)** Furnishing only authentic and verifiable information when exercising the right to correction or erasure.
