## 3. Service Description

3.1. **Overview.** Teloscopy is an open-source, multi-agent genomic intelligence platform that provides the following informational and educational services:

&emsp;&emsp;**(a) Telomere Analysis and Biological Age Estimation** — The Platform analyses quantitative fluorescence in situ hybridization (qFISH) microscopy images to measure telomere lengths at individual chromosome ends and derives an estimated biological age based on published telomere attrition models.

&emsp;&emsp;**(b) Disease Risk Prediction** — Using telomere length data, optional self-reported genetic variants (single nucleotide polymorphisms or SNPs), and user-provided demographic information, the Platform generates statistical risk assessments for conditions including, but not limited to, Alzheimer's disease, cardiovascular disease, various cancers, type 2 diabetes, osteoporosis, and other age-related diseases.

&emsp;&emsp;**(c) Personalised Nutrition and Diet Recommendations** — Based on the User's genomic risk profile, geographic region, dietary restrictions, and caloric requirements, the Platform generates nutrigenomic dietary guidance and multi-day meal plans featuring region-specific foods from thirty-five (35) supported regions.

&emsp;&emsp;**(d) Facial-Genomic Analysis** — The Platform optionally analyses a face photograph to provide: (i) biological age estimation from facial features, (ii) ancestry estimation across a six-population model, (iii) statistical prediction of up to fifty-nine (59) SNP variants, (iv) approximate DNA sequence reconstruction with flanking regions, (v) pharmacogenomic phenotype predictions for up to eight (8) drug metabolism genes, (vi) health screening indicators (BMI, anaemia risk, cardiovascular and thyroid indicators), (vii) dermatological analysis (rosacea, melasma, acne, UV damage), and (viii) ancestry-derived predictions (mitochondrial haplogroup, lactose tolerance, alcohol flush response).

&emsp;&emsp;**(e) Health Checkup Analysis** — The Platform allows Users to upload annual health checkup reports (blood tests, urine tests, and abdomen scan reports) in PDF, image, or text format. The Platform extracts and analyses values across sixty-two (62) blood parameters and thirteen (13) urine parameters, runs twenty-four (24) automated condition detectors, computes a health score (0–100), and generates a tailored dietary plan.

&emsp;&emsp;**(f) Report Generation** — The Platform generates downloadable reports in HTML, JSON, and CSV formats consolidating the above analyses.

3.2. **Nature of the Service.** The Platform is a research-grade informational and educational tool. It is provided for general wellness, informational, and educational purposes only. The Platform is **NOT** a medical service, diagnostic tool, or substitute for professional medical advice. See [Section 4 (Medical Disclaimer)](#4-medical-disclaimer) for comprehensive limitations.

3.3. **No Guarantee of Availability.** As an open-source project, the Platform is provided on an "as-is" and "as-available" basis. We do not guarantee uninterrupted, timely, secure, or error-free operation of the Platform.

&emsp;&emsp;**(c)** The Platform supports import of genotype files from third-party services (including 23andMe, AncestryDNA, and VCF formats), but does not validate the accuracy of the underlying genotyping performed by those services.

5.2. **Facial-to-Genetic Predictions.** The facial-genomic analysis module of the Platform predicts genetic variants from facial photographs using statistical models. Users acknowledge and understand that:

&emsp;&emsp;**(a)** Facial-to-genetic predictions are **statistical estimates** derived from population-level correlations between facial morphology and allele frequencies. They carry **significant uncertainty** and are inherently less reliable than actual genotyping from biological specimens.

&emsp;&emsp;**(b)** Ancestry estimations produced from facial analysis are based on a six-population admixture model and represent broad continental-level approximations. They do not capture the full complexity of human genetic diversity, population substructure, or individual admixture history.

&emsp;&emsp;**(c)** Confidence scores associated with predicted variants reflect model-internal probability estimates and do not represent clinical sensitivity or specificity metrics.

5.3. **DNA Sequence Reconstruction.** The DNA reconstruction feature of the Platform generates approximate nucleotide sequences based on predicted variants and reference genome flanking regions (GRCh38). Users acknowledge and understand that:

&emsp;&emsp;**(a)** Reconstructed DNA sequences are **approximate computational outputs** and are **NOT equivalent to actual DNA sequencing** results obtained from biological specimens through standard sequencing technologies (Sanger sequencing, Illumina short-read sequencing, Oxford Nanopore long-read sequencing, or PacBio sequencing).

&emsp;&emsp;**(b)** Reconstructed sequences should not be submitted to clinical databases, used for clinical interpretation, or relied upon for any health, legal, or identity-related decision.

&emsp;&emsp;**(c)** The FASTA-format output is generated for informational and educational purposes only.

5.4. **No Genetic Counselling.** The Platform does **NOT** provide genetic counselling services.
